Movement-pattern literacy

Train the body by function, not just by muscle.

This pattern layer is what makes the atlas useful across ages and goals: it prevents random exercise selection.

P

Push

Bench, pushup, overhead press. Balance with enough pulling.

R

Pull

Rows, pulldowns, chinups, face pulls. Posture and shoulder health.

S

Squat

Knee-dominant leg strength. Back squat, goblet squat, split squat.

H

Hinge

Posterior-chain strength. Deadlift, RDL, hip raise, swings.

C

Carry/Core

Grip, trunk stiffness, anti-rotation, posture under load.

M

Mobility

Warmups, resets, range preservation, tissue quality.

Teaching workflow

A repeatable lesson structure for clients.

This turns the site from a reference into a consulting tool: every lesson becomes transparent, structured, and documentable.

STEP 01

Assess

Choose a track, identify equipment, note injuries, and observe squat, hinge, push, pull, and plank basics.

STEP 02

Teach

Start with a main move and one regression. Explain target muscles, movement pattern, and key safety cues.

STEP 03

Train

Use two or three exercises only. Record sets, reps, load, and subjective quality.

STEP 04

Progress

Advance by adding range, control, reps, or load—never by sacrificing form.

Safety note. This prototype is educational. Clients should train within their competence, use maintained equipment, and seek medical or professional guidance when appropriate.
